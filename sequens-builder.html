The six-part spine.

Every lesson the builder makes follows the same evidence-based shape. The renderer enforces it: the model cannot skip a step, and neither can a rushed Thursday afternoon.

1First thing

Retrieval from last time. Memory is strengthened by being used.

2Show them how

Worked example, thinking aloud, step by step.

3Build it together

Guided practice before independence.

4Quick check

The hinge question targets a named misconception before it embeds.

5Their turn

One shared task, entered at the right level.

6Last thing

Recap and exit ticket. The learning leaves the room named.

Every plan opens with its place in the sequence: the prior objective, this one, the next. A lesson is part of a coherent unit, not a one-off.

Because every lesson follows the same shape, a whole staff builds from one consistent structure - which is also what a school's SEQUENS coaching works from. The lesson builder sits inside the school arm, not beside it.

Adaptive teaching, not tiered worksheets.

Their turn is one shared task entered at the right level. The builder generates responsive scaffold and stretch around it: vocabulary pre-taught, sentence stems, a picture key for the children who need a way in; depth, not more of the same, for the children who are ready.

Stretch moves a child toward age-related expectation without overloading working memory. Scaffolds print ink-light, for books. And because generation is live, a scaffold can be shaped to the actual child: an interest that motivates them, a symbol per step for early English, one instruction at a time for a child who needs it.

On a pupil's slide there is only a colour. The words live on the teacher key.

Your school sets the marking policy; SEQUENS provides the proof that feedback happened and what came next.

What the research is clear about.

The builder is engineered on this research. Each finding below is built into the engine.

Memory is strengthened by retrieval, not re-reading.+

Recalling knowledge changes it: retrieval practice produces more durable learning than reviewing the same material again, and spacing those retrievals multiplies the effect.

SEQUENS builds in retrieval: every lesson opens with First thing, retrieval from last time, and closes with Last thing. Coverage feeds revisit, so what was secured comes back before it fades.
Roediger & Karpicke · EEF cognitive science review
Novices learn from worked examples and clear modelling.+

New learning lands when it is shown, step by step, before it is attempted; guided practice bridges the gap to independence. Asking novices to discover unaided overloads them.

SEQUENS models before independence: Show them how and Build it together are enforced stages of the spine, with the thinking made visible before Their turn begins.
Sweller, worked-example effect · Rosenshine, principles of instruction
Checking understanding beats assuming it.+

Formative checks change teaching in the moment. A well-built hinge question exposes the specific misconception a class is most likely to hold, while there is still time to act on it.

SEQUENS checks understanding: the Quick check is a hinge targeting a named misconception, generated with the lesson. Coverage is never mistaken for learning.
Wiliam & Black, inside the black box · EEF feedback guidance
One shared task, entered at the right level, beats three worksheets.+

Working memory is small; overload it and nothing sticks. Adaptive scaffolds give every child a way into the same learning, and stretch adds depth toward age-related expectation rather than a separate, capped track.

SEQUENS keeps it one task: Their turn is one shared task with responsive scaffold and stretch generated around it. Labels stay on the teacher key, never on a child's screen.
Cognitive-load theory · EEF SEND guidance, scaffolding
We think in words and pictures, so teaching uses both.+

Pairing a clear explanation with a simple, matched visual lets more be held and more be kept than either channel alone.

SEQUENS is dual coded: the seven Frames put a picture beside the thinking, on slides and plans, wherever it earns its place.
Paivio, dual coding · Mayer, multimedia learning · Caviglioli
Inclusion is a foundation, not a feature.+

Reaching every learner, including children with SEND and children learning English, is the condition on the whole design, not an option beside it.

SEQUENS is inclusive by design: scaffolds carry symbol support and chunked instructions where a child needs them, dual coding runs through every deck, and personalisation shapes the same learning to the actual child.
EEF SEND guidance · dual-coding research · the SEQUENS engine, adaptive by default

Safe by design.

Generation needs no pupil personal data. The teacher holds the account, plans are built from objectives, and nothing about a child has to leave the school to make a lesson.

Every generated artefact carries the review marker, because the teacher is the author and the final check. Video is off by default and embeds only after an adult previews and confirms, in privacy mode. There is no pupil-facing AI.

Because a lesson is designed to be taught from the board and printed for books, the classroom never depends on live connectivity mid-lesson.

And because the teacher reviews every lesson before it is taught, content stays appropriate to your school's setting and cultural context - the teacher is the final check on what fits the class in front of them.
