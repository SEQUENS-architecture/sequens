Why this sheet is shaped like this. The steps to success at the top are the same four sentences that are on the board, and the same four the teacher confirms at the end. The child ticks them themselves, which is the only assessment on this sheet that belongs to them.

Question 2 is the Turn. Their own wrong idea, in their own writing, and then their own corrected one underneath it. When they look back at this page in a month, they can see themselves change their mind.

The starters are offered, never assigned. They are printed on every sheet, so no child can be identified by what they were given. The reading-support version is also the same sheet - warmer paper, wider spacing - so a child who needs it is not holding a different-looking piece of paper from everyone else.
