Eleven visual organisers, one per thinking procedure. Dual-coded into decks and plans wherever a picture will carry more than words alone. A child meets the same shape in Year 2 and in Year 9, and recognises it instantly. That consistency is the whole point.

Two rules that keep the library honest

1. The engine chooses. It never invents. Frames are thinking procedures, not decoration. Their value is recognition across years and subjects. If an engine improvised a new shape per lesson, that dies, and you have clip art rather than an architecture. The engine picks from these eleven, and it picks the one that matches the cognitive move.

2. No frame is a legitimate answer. If none of the eleven fits the thinking, the lesson uses none. A missing frame is honest. A bolted-on frame is a small lie, and it teaches children that the shapes mean nothing.
