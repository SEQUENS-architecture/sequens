Year 4 · Science

Classifying living things

~50 min · I can use a classification key to sort living things into groups.

✦ AI-assisted · you review before use
Sequence: ← Grouping by observable features  ·  Now: classification keys  ·  → Environments & change →    part of a coherent unit, not a one-off
First thing

Sort & justify

Show six animals. "Sort these into two groups - how did you decide?" Listen for the thinking before naming the rule.

Show me how

Model the key

Build a key aloud: "Has a backbone?" → vertebrate / invertebrate. Structure, not appearance.

Do it together

Classify the frog

Walk the key as a class, voting at each yes/no question before revealing.

Quick check · hinge

Catch the misconception

"A snake is long and bendy with no legs - vertebrate or invertebrate?" If many say invertebrate → re-model: looks ≠ structure.

Their turn

Sort independently

One shared task, entered at the right level - see the adaptive support to the right.

Last thing

Recall

Hands show backbone / no backbone. Exit: name one vertebrate and one invertebrate.

Working towards Secure Greater depth
Class starts as "met" - tap the few who didn't, or who went beyond.
Working towards

Recognises some equivalent fractions using concrete materials or visual representations. May need support to explain the relationship between the numerator and denominator. Can identify equivalent pairs in familiar contexts (e.g. 1/2 and 2/4 with a bar model shown).

Secure

Recognises and can show equivalent fractions using visual representations. Understands that multiplying (or dividing) the numerator and denominator by the same number creates an equivalent fraction. Can generate equivalent fractions and name them (e.g. 2/4, 3/6, 4/8 are all equal to 1/2).

Greater depth

Applies understanding of equivalent fractions to reason about and compare fractions. Explains the relationship between fractions with different denominators (e.g. "3/4 is more than 2/3 because..."). Can work backwards: given a fraction, name all equivalent fractions to a certain limit, or reason about which is the simplest form.

Descriptors derived from England NC (2014) and typical Year 4 progression. If a child doesn't fit one level cleanly, mark the best fit - the record captures the conversation, not the label.
